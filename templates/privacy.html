01

Overview

This Privacy Policy describes how Nexus AI ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, stores, and shares information when you use our web-based AI assistant service (the "Service") at nexusai.

We are committed to protecting your privacy and handling your data with transparency and care. This policy applies to all users of the Service and is incorporated by reference into our Terms of Service.

By using the Service, you consent to the data practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with this policy, please do not use the Service.

02

Data We Collect

Nexus AI is designed with privacy in mind. We collect only the minimum data necessary to provide and improve the Service:

Data You Provide Directly:

  • Prompts & Input Text — The questions, text, and instructions you type into the chat interface. This content is sent to third-party LLM providers to generate responses.
  • Feedback Ratings — If you click "Yes" or "No" on the feedback buttons after a response, we store this rating along with the associated prompt, response, selected function, and style.

Data Collected Automatically:

  • Server Logs — Standard web server logs may include your IP address, browser type, device information, pages visited, and timestamps. These are used for security monitoring and debugging.
  • Session Data — Temporary in-browser session data (such as conversation history) is stored locally in your browser's memory and is not transmitted to or stored on our servers beyond the current session.

Data We Do NOT Collect:

  • We do not require account registration or collect email addresses, names, or personal identifiers.
  • We do not use third-party analytics platforms (such as Google Analytics).
  • We do not collect payment information of any kind.
  • We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties.
03

How We Use Your Data

The data we collect is used exclusively for the following purposes:

  • Providing the Service — Your prompts are forwarded to third-party LLM APIs to generate AI responses. This is the core function of Nexus AI and cannot be disabled.
  • Improving Response Quality — Voluntarily submitted feedback (thumbs up/down ratings) is stored in a local feedback.json file and may be used to assess and improve prompt templates and model selection.
  • Security & Abuse Prevention — Server logs are used to detect and prevent unauthorized access, abuse, or security threats.
  • Debugging & Performance — Technical error data and provider response logs are used internally to diagnose and fix issues with the Service.

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, or any commercial purpose beyond delivering the Service.

04

Data Storage & Retention

Feedback Data: Feedback you submit (ratings and associated conversation data) is stored locally in a feedback.json file on the server. This data is retained indefinitely unless manually deleted by the system administrator. Each feedback entry contains:

  • Timestamp of submission
  • The selected function (Q&A, Summarize, Creative) and prompt style
  • Your input prompt and the AI-generated response
  • Your rating (helpful: true/false)

Conversation History: Your chat conversation is maintained temporarily in your browser's JavaScript memory for the duration of your session. This data is not transmitted to our servers and is permanently lost when you close or refresh the page.

Nexus AI does not use a database. All persistent storage is file-based and local to the server instance. No cloud databases, third-party data warehouses, or external storage services are used for user data.
05

Third-Party AI Providers

When you submit a prompt, it is transmitted to one of the following AI providers to generate a response. Each provider has its own data handling practices:

  1. Groq — Groq Privacy Policy. Groq processes your prompt to generate completions. Refer to their policy for data retention details.
  2. Google Gemini — Google Privacy Policy. Subject to Google's standard data processing terms for API usage.
  3. Cohere — Cohere Privacy Policy. Cohere may process API requests in accordance with their enterprise data handling policies.
  4. HuggingFace — HuggingFace Privacy Policy. Inference API requests are subject to HuggingFace's terms and data practices.
Important: When you submit a prompt, it is sent to these third-party providers. Do not include sensitive personal information (passwords, financial data, government IDs, medical records) in your prompts. We cannot control how third-party providers handle transmitted data.
06

Cookies & Tracking

Nexus AI uses minimal browser storage technologies:

  • No tracking cookies — We do not use advertising, analytics, or cross-site tracking cookies.
  • Session memory — Conversation history is held in JavaScript memory (not localStorage or cookies) and is cleared when the page is closed.
  • No third-party trackers — We do not embed any third-party tracking scripts, pixels, or SDKs.

External resources loaded by the Service include Google Fonts and Font Awesome icons. Loading these resources involves your browser making requests to their CDNs, which may involve standard IP logging by those providers.

07

Security Measures

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical security measures to protect information from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction:

  • HTTPS encryption — All communications between your browser and the Service are encrypted via HTTPS/TLS when deployed with SSL.
  • API key security — Third-party LLM API keys are stored in environment variables, not in source code or version control.
  • No sensitive data storage — We avoid storing sensitive personal data, reducing the risk surface of a potential breach.
  • Rate limiting — Built-in provider rate limit handling prevents excessive API usage.

However, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security of information transmitted to or stored by the Service.

08

Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of Access — You may request information about what data we hold about you (e.g., feedback entries linked to your session).
  • Right to Deletion — You may request deletion of any feedback data you submitted. Contact us using the details in Section 12.
  • Right to Correction — You may request correction of inaccurate data we hold about you.
  • Right to Object — You may object to certain processing activities. Note that objecting to core data processing may prevent use of the Service.
  • Right to Portability — Where technically feasible, you may request a copy of your data in a structured, machine-readable format.

Because Nexus AI does not require user accounts, many of these rights are limited in practice — we may not be able to identify feedback entries as belonging to a specific individual without additional context provided by you.

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or California, you have additional rights under GDPR, UK GDPR, and CCPA respectively. Please contact us for more information about exercising these rights.
09

Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 years of age.

If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child under 13 has used the Service or submitted feedback data, please contact us immediately using the details in Section 12. We will take prompt steps to delete such information from our records.

Users between the ages of 13 and 17 should use the Service only with parental or guardian consent, as outlined in our Terms of Service.

10

International Users

Nexus AI is operated from India. If you are accessing the Service from outside India, please be aware that your information may be transferred to, stored, and processed in India or in countries where our third-party LLM providers operate their servers.

By using the Service, you consent to the transfer of your information to countries that may not provide the same level of data protection as your home country. We take steps to ensure appropriate safeguards are in place for such transfers.

  • EU/EEA Users: Prompts sent to LLM providers may be processed outside the EEA. By using the Service, you acknowledge this cross-border transfer.
  • California Users (CCPA): We do not sell your personal information. You have the right to know what data we collect and to request deletion.
